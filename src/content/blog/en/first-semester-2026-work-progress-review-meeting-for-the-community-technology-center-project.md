Phnom Penh: On July 15, 2026, at the Ministry of Post and Telecommunications (MPTC), H.E. Mao Sela, Undersecretary of State of the MPTC and Chairman of the Community Technology Center (CTC) Working Group, chaired a meeting to review and assess the work progress of the Community Technology Center project for the first semester of 2026. The meeting was attended by H.E. Dr. San Vathana, Secretary of State of the Ministry of Education, Youth and Sports (MoEYS), alongside relevant working groups.

The purpose of this meeting was to track the progress of project implementation, review achieved results, discuss challenges, and set strategic directions for the upcoming semester. Discussions focused primarily on center operations, training programs, and other related tasks to ensure that project implementation proceeds efficiently and meets the goals of enhancing digital capabilities within communities.

As a result, the working groups from both the MPTC and MoEYS agreed on the next steps and action plans to further drive project implementation. They also committed to strengthening cooperation between the relevant institutions to achieve their shared goal of supporting digital human capital development, promoting digital literacy, and reducing the digital divide in communities.

Note on the Project:
The Community Technology Centers were initiated and constructed by the Ministry of Post and Telecommunications (MPTC) in collaboration with the Ministry of Education, Youth and Sports (MoEYS). The initiative aims to provide digital skills training, promote digital literacy, and reduce the digital divide within communities in an inclusive and innovative manner.

Each CTC consists of 4 main functions:

(1) Computer Lab

(2) Training Room

(3) Post Office

(4) Public Hall
