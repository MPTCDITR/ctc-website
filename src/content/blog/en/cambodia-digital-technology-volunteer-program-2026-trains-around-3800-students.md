Cambodia Digital Technology Volunteer Program 2026 Trains Around 3,800 Students
The Cambodia Academy of Digital Technology (CADT), under the Ministry of Post and Telecommunications, has relaunched the Cambodia Digital Technology Volunteer 2026 (CDTV 2026) program. In collaboration with the Community Tech Center (CTC), 10 CADT student volunteers have been deployed to 5 high schools in Takeo Province from July 1st to July 31st, 2026.

Program Background
The Cambodia Digital Technology Volunteer program was initiated and established in 2015, following a cooperation with South Korea's National Information Society Agency (NIA) and the International Telecommunication Union (ITU).

With full support from the Ministry of Post and Telecommunications, the program has been reintroduced under the new name CDTV. For this phase, it focuses on 5 high schools in Takeo Province:

- Hun Sen 1st June High School

- Heng Samrin Prey Lvea High School

- Sok An Thnal Bombek High School

- Hun Sen Koh Andet High School

- Sok An Tonloab High School

Core Objectives & Curriculum
The 10 CADT student volunteers aim to teach approximately 3,800 students across various essential digital subjects, including:

- Scratch programming

- Digital design

- Computer basics

- Algorithms

- Data analysis

- Digital safety

- Artificial Intelligence (AI)

Future Vision
Timeline & Goals: This training period runs from July 1–31, 2026. Looking ahead, the program envisions expanding to 45 schools across 10 target provinces, aiming to reach 18,000 students by 2030.

As a reminder, since its inception in 2015, the program has successfully trained over 6,769 Cambodians through the efforts of 178 volunteers across 3 provinces.
