The Community Tech Center (CTC) provides training related to digital skills to high school students, civil servants, business owners, farmers, and local citizens. The training aims to improve their understanding of digital technologies so they can effectively use these tools to support their daily lives. It also enables them to access and utilize government public services through online platforms.

The Community Tech Center (CTC) has four main facilities: a Computer Lab, a Training Room, a Post Office, and a Public Hall, which includes an Internet room and an Internet café.

At present, the Ministry of Post and Telecommunications (MPTC) has established and operationalized 21 Community Tech Centers nationwide. The Ministry continues to develop strategic plans for further expansion to enhance access to digital services and training across the country.
